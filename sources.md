# Sources, Filters, and Scoring

Goal: a wide net of best-fit growth roles, ranked, not gated. Fit is a tier with a reason, never an auto-reject (except hard disqualifiers).

## Sources to scrape

**VC job boards**
- Y Combinator: https://www.ycombinator.com/jobs/role/marketing
- a16z: https://jobs.a16z.com/jobs?jobTypes=Marketing
- Kleiner Perkins: https://jobs.kleinerperkins.com/companies
- Sequoia (via topstartups feed), Bessemer, First Round, Greylock talent networks (Getro / Consider powered)

**Aggregators**
- TopStartups: https://topstartups.io/?funding_round=Series%20A (and Series B, Seed). Click "see more" to load the full list.
- Wellfound (AngelList Talent), Built In, Y Combinator Work at a Startup

**Direct career pages (ATS)**
- Ashby, Lever, Greenhouse, Workable boards for target companies (e.g., LangChain on Ashby).

## Roles in scope (titles to match, broadly)
Growth Marketer, Growth Lead, Founding Growth, Head of Growth, Growth Marketing Manager, Product Marketing Manager (PMM), Lifecycle / Retention Marketing, Content / Content-led Growth, Demand Gen, Conversion / CRO, Web / Landing, Growth Engineer, GTM / RevOps-adjacent marketing, AI Product/Growth marketing.

## Soft ICP (used to rank, not to exclude)
- B2B SaaS preferred. B2B tooling, dev tools, AI products all strong.
- Stage: seed through pre-IPO and post-IPO all in scope.
- US-based or remote-friendly to US.
- Bonus signals: job mentions experimentation, conversion, A/B testing, scrappy, 0-to-1, "wear many hats," founder-led, content.
- Lighter fit (still include, tag as Stretch): heavily regulated or deep verticals (bio, pharma, fintech compliance), very large enterprises, pure brand/PR roles, pure performance/ads-only roles.

## Hard disqualifiers (the only auto-excludes)
- "US citizens only" or explicit "no visa sponsorship, now or ever" (Eddie is on an O-1, which transfers without the H-1B lottery; only exclude when they bar non-citizens outright).
- Not a marketing/growth role at all.
- Location-locked to a region Eddie can't work in.

## Fit tiers (the ranking)
- **Strong:** B2B SaaS, early-to-mid stage, role explicitly wants experimentation / conversion / systems / scrappy generalist. Eddie stands out.
- **Good:** solid B2B SaaS or adjacent growth role; clear overlap, some gaps.
- **Stretch:** real but partial fit (vertical, very senior, or narrow specialist). Worth a shot, especially with strong outreach.

## Stage tags
Seed, Series A, Series B, Series C+, Pre-IPO, Public/Post-IPO, Post-acquisition.

## Also capture
- Great-fit companies with no open role (for proactive outreach).
- For each: company, role, source, link, stage, fit tier + one-line reason, founder/hiring-manager name if findable, and an outreach signal (a feature, a traffic trend, a weak demo page, a content gap).
