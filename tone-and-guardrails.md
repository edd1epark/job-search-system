# Tone and Guardrails

Every resume bullet, intro line, and outreach message must follow these.

## Voice
- Clear over clever. Plain words, short sentences, roughly a 6th to 8th grade reading level.
- Punchy and accomplishment-first. Lead with the result, then how. Quantify wherever real.
- Write from the founder's chair: what stage are they at, what does that stage need, what would make this hire obvious, what objection might they have.

## Formatting
- Bold key accomplishments, metrics, and decisions/insights, NOT tool names. Bolding every tool makes bullets hard to scan. Examples to bold: "$103K in revenue", "sourced 40% of deals", "improved ROAS by 45.47%", "the harder call: go in-house". Tools are listed (unbolded) in a Tools section instead.
- Never use em-dashes or en-dashes. Use a plain hyphen, a colon, or restructure the sentence.
- No standalone Summary or Skills section. The value should be clear from a 10-second scan of the experience.
- Each resume opens with a one-line intro personalized to the company.

## Avoid (AI-tells and weak moves)
- The staccato negation pattern ("Not X. Not Y. Z.") - use very sparingly, at most once.
- The word "operator" as a label, and generic claims any growth marketer could copy. Test every line: could a competitor paste it onto their resume? If yes, cut or sharpen it.
- Overstuffed buzzwords. One strong concrete fact beats three adjectives.

## Honesty
- Tell the pivot story concisely so the founder thinks "he made a hard decision and grew from it."
- Be honest about what did not work and the lesson. Never lead with the runway/financial detail.
- Draw the connection explicitly: when an experience is not an obvious fit, say in one line why it matters for this role.
- When tailoring per role, trim peripheral bullets and re-order to lead with what matters. Keep the narrative spine (the pivot story) intact - never water it down to a vague line.
